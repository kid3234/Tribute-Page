Biography

Tsegaye Gabre-Medhin, a prominent Ethiopian playwright and poet, was born in Bodaa village near Addis Ababa. He wrote plays from a young age and later studied theater in Europe. Gabre-Medhin played a significant role in managing the Ethiopian National Theater and received accolades, including the Haile Selassie I Prize for Amharic Literature. His works celebrated Ethiopia's history and cultural heritage, with plays like "Tewodros" and "Petros at the Hour" gaining international recognition.
